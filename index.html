1. Ethics

participants are humans. They have a right to be treated with dignity and, whenever possible, to gain some benefit from the research. They should not be ‘used’ simply for the researcher’s own ends

Researching information systems and computing by B.J. Oates (2006)

The rights of participants

  • Not to participate
  • To withdraw
  • To give informed consent
  • To anonymity
  • To confidentiality

Researching information systems and computing by B.J. Oates (2006)

2. Research question(s)

Useful research questions are

  • Focused, narrow, specific
  • Answerable
  • Feasible
  • Open (what, why, how)

a research question

Why are FOSS contributors having trouble submitting session proposals to speak at FOSDEM?

Break it down

  • Can they find the submission system?
  • Can they identify the right conference track?
  • Do they understand the different submission form fields?
  • Do they know when the submission process is complete?
  • Do they understand what happens after submitting?

How many?

Elaborate usability tests are a waste of resources. The best results come from testing no more than 5 users and running as many small tests as you can afford.

Why You Only Need to Test with 5 Users by J. Nielsen (2000)

With a caveat

Purposive sample (…) size typically relies on the concept of “saturation”, or the point at which no new information or themes are observed in the data.

How Many Interviews Are Enough? An Experiment with Data Saturation and Variability, by G. Guest, A. Bunce and L. Johnson (2006)

1.1 Unmoderated vs. moderated

  • Moderated!!!! (no brainer)
  • Moderator AND note taker
  • Think aloud protocol

Think aloud protocol

In a thinking aloud test, you ask test participants to use the system while continuously thinking out loud — that is, simply verbalizing their thoughts as they move through the user interface.

Usability Engineering, by J. Nielsen (1993)

1.2 Remote vs. co-located

  • Aim for co-located
  • Realistically, remote for FOSS
  • Video calling with screen sharing
    • Web-based (no installation)
    • Jitsi (FOSS)
    • Closed options (Hangouts, Skype, Zoom)

5. Introductory Interview

Sample interview

  • Introduce yourself: Who are you? What do you do?
  • Which conferences have you spoken at?
  • Which subjects do you normally present about?
  • What was the last conference you spoke at?
  • Tell us about the submission process
  • What makes a good submission process?
  • Any other thoughts?
